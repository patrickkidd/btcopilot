## Unresolved Ambiguities

### 1. Conflict vs. Distance — Operational Definitions (new — major)

**Statements:** Arthur 2-3
**The question:** Where is the boundary between conflict and distance as coding categories?
**Positions:**
- **Laura:** Conflict = a difference where neither party gives in, including passive resistance, stonewalling, and Cold War scenarios. Distance = true avoidance/disengagement ("I'm just not going to deal with it"). Digging in heels (even silently) is conflict, not distance. She coded Arthur-Elena as conflict.
- **Patrick:** Questioned whether there's evidence Arthur is engaging/standing ground — looks like disengagement. Previously considered Cold War as "distance from both sides." Found Laura's arguments "helpful" but didn't shift.
- **Guillermo:** Offered "distance as a position in conflict" — distance as the outcome of unresolved conflict. Laura disagreed: they are two different mechanisms.
**What would resolve it:** Literature review of Bowen's mechanism enumeration (Laura searching her Kindle). Possibly more cases with clearer examples. This is the most substantive theoretical disagreement in the study so far.

### 2. Defined Self — Scope and Inference Threshold (new)

**Statement:** Arthur 1
**The question:** When can defined self be inferred from limited evidence? Does it require calm/rational action or can it be emotionally driven?
**Positions:**
- **Laura:** Defined self is "a move" — action taken in response to the other. Can be done at any level of functioning. 27 variables in differentiation; defining self is just one. Wife sending husband to therapy = defining self based on clinical pattern.
- **Patrick:** Conservative — "depends on how she said it, what her motivation was." Not enough evidence from one statement. But acknowledged corrections can happen over time.
**What would resolve it:** Literature review on defined self. More cases with clearer examples.

### 3. Symptom vs. Anxiety for Presenting Problems (new)

**Statement:** Arthur 1
**The question:** When a client presents with a non-clinical complaint ("somewhat unsettled"), is it symptom or anxiety?
**Positions:**
- **Patrick:** Presenting problem as symptom (working approach).
- **Kathy/Guillermo:** Anxiety up — "nobody was clinical."
**What would resolve it:** Operational definitions for symptom vs. anxiety boundary. Literature review.

### 4. Face-Value Reporting (new)

**Statement:** Arthur 2
**The question:** When a speaker characterizes another person's emotions (e.g., "she worries"), should coders accept this at face value or apply skepticism?
**Positions:**
- **Laura:** Skeptical — from experience, the wife may be aggravated, not worried. "I tended to be skeptical about what he says about why she's doing anything."
- **Patrick:** Two approaches (accept and refine vs. wait for evidence). Goes back and forth.
**What would resolve it:** Group decision on methodology. Possibly a rule: code as stated with a confidence marker, or code as "best assumption."
