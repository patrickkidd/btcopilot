### How Context Beyond Excerpts Informed Interpretation

The 95 indexed passages were selected FROM these full sequential reads. The surrounding context informed interpretation even when not explicitly excerpted:

1. **Bowen's terminology mapping** - Reading FTiCP Ch 9 in full revealed that "overadequate-inadequate" is Bowen's term for what FE Ch 7 calls "overfunctioning-underfunctioning." Grep for "function*" would miss this connection.

2. **The "borrowing" mechanism requires full narrative** - FE Ch 4's explanation that one person can "borrow" functional self from another (gaining strength from their adaptation) only makes sense reading the full passage flow, not isolated sentences.

3. **FTiCP Ch 21's three-step family reaction** - The "You are wrong → Change back → Consequences" sequence appears across several paragraphs with examples; a grep snippet would capture one step but miss the full pattern.

4. **Negative definitions emerged from contrast** - Understanding that "high F is NOT emotional coldness" came from reading Bowen's extended discussion of maintaining emotional contact while differentiated—not from any single quotable sentence.

5. **Scale range nuances** - FTiCP Ch 9's description of 0-25 vs 25-50 vs 50-75 vs 75-100 ranges required reading the full section to see how behaviors shift across the continuum.

### Key Conceptual Discoveries from Full Reads

**From FTiCP Ch 21 (most significant):**
- Bowen's detailed personal narrative provided the richest behavioral examples
- "A differentiated self is one who can maintain emotional objectivity while in the midst of an emotional system in turmoil, yet at the same time actively relate to key people in the system" (FT21-35)
- The three-step family reaction to differentiation: "You are wrong," "Change back," "If you do not, these are the consequences" (FT21-25)
- Differentiation requires ACTION in meaningful relationships, not just insight
- "Differentiation cannot take place in a vacuum" (FT21-30)

**From FE Ch 7:**
- Detailed mechanisms of overfunctioning-underfunctioning reciprocity
- How the "functional spouse" views the dysfunctional one as "sick"
- Sibling F levels interact: "one child can do 'everything right' in reaction to another child's doing 'everything wrong'"

**From FTiCP Ch 9:**
- Most explicit scale range descriptions
- "People in the lower half of the scale live in a 'feeling' controlled world"
- "do not distinguish feeling from fact, and major life decisions are based on what 'feels' right"

### Cross-Reference Analysis Results

**Convergences (all 5 sources agree):**
1. Basic vs. functional distinction - stable basic, fluctuating functional
2. Reciprocity - F in one person affects F in others
3. Feeling vs. thinking as core discriminator
4. I-position as marker of higher functioning
5. F and anxiety are inversely related
6. F shifts follow family system shifts

**Tensions/Nuances:**
1. Havstad operationalizes F for research; others treat it more descriptively
2. FTiCP Ch 21 emphasizes action/experience; FE Ch 4 emphasizes cognitive distinction
3. FE Ch 4 vs FTiCP Ch 9 have slightly different scale range descriptions

### Gaps and Uncertainties Identified

1. **Detection window unclear** - Sources describe F shifts over "hours" to "months" but don't specify minimum observable window for transcript classification
2. **Temporary vs consolidated** - How to distinguish momentary fluctuation from stable functional position?
3. **Self-report reliability** - Sources suggest low-F individuals lack insight into their own functioning level
4. **Non-family contexts** - Sources focus on family; applicability to work/social contexts implied but not elaborated

### Negative Definitions Constructed

Critical for classification - what F is NOT:
1. F is NOT basic differentiation (basic is fixed)
2. F is NOT intelligence or education
3. F is NOT the ability to CLAIM a self position (angry claiming = low F)
4. F is NOT the same as feeling good/stable
5. F shifts are NOT permanent changes in basic level
6. High F is NOT emotional coldness
7. Differentiation is NOT selfishness
